What is F1?

Formula 1 is the highest class of international racing for single-seater formula racing cars. It is known for cutting-edge technology, extreme speeds, and global competition.

The championship consists of races called Grands Prix, held worldwide on purpose-built circuits and public roads.

Famous Teams

Some of the most iconic teams include Ferrari, Mercedes, and Red Bull Racing. These teams have dominated different eras of Formula 1.

Each team designs and builds its own car, constantly improving performance through innovation and engineering.

Tracks

F1 races take place on famous tracks like Monaco, Silverstone, and Spa-Francorchamps.

Each track has unique challenges including sharp corners and long straights.

Speed & Technology

F1 cars are among the most advanced machines in the world, featuring hybrid engines and aerodynamics.

They can exceed speeds of 350 km/h.
